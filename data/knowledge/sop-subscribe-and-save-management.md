# SOP: Subscribe & Save Management

## Purpose
This SOP outlines the standard process for managing Subscribe & Save (S&S) programs across client accounts. S&S is a critical revenue driver for consumable products — supplements, skincare, and pet wellness brands typically see 25–40% of total revenue from subscriptions.

## When to Use
- Weekly S&S health check (every Monday)
- When a client's S&S cancellation rate exceeds 3% in any given week
- When launching a new ASIN into the S&S program
- During Monthly Business Reviews (subscription section)

## Key Metrics to Monitor

### Healthy Benchmarks
| Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|--------|-------|--------|-----|
| Active subscriber growth (MoM) | > 5% | 2–5% | < 2% or negative |
| Weekly cancellation rate | < 2% | 2–3% | > 3% |
| S&S revenue as % of total | > 30% | 20–30% | < 20% |
| New subscriber acquisition (weekly) | > 3% of active base | 1–3% | < 1% |

### Common Causes of Subscription Churn
1. **Price increases** — even $1–2 increases cause cancellation spikes within 2 weeks
2. **Out-of-stock events** — if a subscriber's order can't be fulfilled, Amazon auto-cancels after 2 missed deliveries
3. **Negative reviews surge** — a wave of 1-star reviews (especially about product changes like taste/smell/texture) drives cancellations
4. **Competitor coupons** — aggressive competitor discounting pulls subscribers away
5. **Seasonal patterns** — January and post-holiday months see natural churn as customers reassess subscriptions

## Response Protocol

### When Cancellation Rate Hits Yellow (2–3%)
1. Pull the cancellation data by ASIN — identify which products are losing subscribers
2. Check for recent price changes, stockouts, or review trends
3. Report findings to the account manager
4. Recommend a 5–10% coupon on the affected ASIN to stabilize

### When Cancellation Rate Hits Red (> 3%)
1. Immediately flag to the Account Director and CEO
2. Pull 30-day trend data to confirm it's not a one-day anomaly
3. Cross-reference with inventory data — is there a fulfillment issue?
4. Cross-reference with review velocity — are new negative reviews spiking?
5. Prepare a client-facing summary with root cause and recommended action
6. Schedule an ad-hoc call with the client if the cause requires their input (e.g., product reformulation)

## S&S Discount Strategy

| Product Price Point | Recommended S&S Discount | Notes |
|--------------------|-----------------------|-------|
| Under $20 | 5% | Margin-sensitive; keep discount minimal |
| $20–$35 | 10% | Sweet spot for conversion |
| Over $35 | 10–15% | Higher discount justified by LTV |

Never exceed 15% S&S discount without CEO approval. The goal is to acquire subscribers profitably, not buy them.

## New ASIN S&S Launch Checklist
- [ ] Confirm ASIN is FBA and Prime-eligible
- [ ] Verify 30+ days of consistent inventory (no stockout risk)
- [ ] Set initial S&S discount (per table above)
- [ ] Enable S&S in Seller Central under the ASIN's offer tab
- [ ] Add "Subscribe & Save" callout to A+ content if not already present
- [ ] Monitor first 2 weeks daily for subscriber acquisition rate
- [ ] Adjust discount at Day 14 based on acquisition data

## Reporting
Include the following in every Monthly Business Review:
- Active subscribers trend (line chart, trailing 6 months)
- S&S revenue as % of total revenue
- Top 3 ASINs by subscriber count
- Any ASINs with cancellation rate above 2%
- Recommendation section: keep, adjust discount, or escalate
